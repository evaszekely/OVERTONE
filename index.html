Relevant Media Coverage

Synthetic Voices Shed Light on the Deep-Rooted Gender Biases Embedded in our Tech (Smithsonian)
View Document

An overview of how synthetic voice design has long mirrored social hierarchies, tracing the history of gendered voice synthesis from aviation to today’s virtual assistants, and showing how technical design choices continue to reproduce ideas of authority and service through sound—issues central to understanding bias embedded in voice technologies.

The Impact of Internet Technology on Women's Voices (NYT)
View Document

A cultural exploration of why so many digital systems speak in female voices, examining how expectations of warmth, patience, and compliance are built into their design, and how these vocal portrayals both reflect and influence attitudes toward women’s voices in everyday interaction.

Sam Altman: People Are Starting to Talk Like AI (Business Insider)
View Document

A discussion of how exposure to AI-generated speech and writing is beginning to affect human language, showing subtle shifts in vocabulary and tone that suggest people are unconsciously aligning with machine-produced styles—an example of how interaction with AI may reshape expressive and social norms in communication.
